My Personal Reflection On Stress Management

What is Stress ??


" Life is 10% what Happens To Us and 90% How We React To It."


Stress, to me, means feeling helpless in tough situations. I get anxious quickly and find it hard to think clearly. Sometimes I lose control over my thoughts and emotions. My confidence drops and I start overthinking everything. But now, I’m realizing stress isn’t my enemy. It’s my signal. It tells me something needs care, space, and attention. It’s something I’m still learning to manage.

My Stress Triggers


Home : Ongoing personal and family problems impact the comfort I associate with home.


Exams and Result : The pressure to meet expectations during exams often weighs heavily on my mind.


GTU Fighter: When my name comes in the GTU fighter list, it makes me feel more stressed.


Bond disturbance: When my friends feel hurt or upset, and their changing behavior creates emotional distance, it deeply affects me..


Relatives : When They came at My Home. and especially, it increases more when our housekepeer is on leave.


Negative overthinking: Sometimes, I just cant stop myself from thinking Negatively.


Other : Anything that occured unexpectadly.

My Stress Management Techniques


Talking to friends & Nanima : I like to share my day,thoughts to my friends and my Grandmother. It feels me relaxed and Lighter


Night walking: I like going for a walk at night when everything is quiet. The silence help me clear my thoughts and feel peaceful.


Cooking food: Making food by myself helps me feel relaxed,mind busy and gives me a break from stress. I enjoy the process and it makes me feel better.


Room No.111: I feel very relaxed while just sitting outside Room 111 in the hostel and doing any work or watching the moon.


Diary Writting: When my mind feels heavy or happy - I write or makes small drawings.


ABC Analysis Technique: When I heard it from PLSD Session. I started Applying it and getting it so helful.


Reverse To-Do List: Making it reduces my level of stress.

Areas of Improvement


I have noticed that I easily get affected by overthinking, fear of failure, and poor time management. These habits increase my stress levels and disturb my focus. One major area where I need to improve is in handling emotional triggers calmly instead of reacting immediately. I also often skip rest or delay my meals during busy days, which affects both my physical and mental health. Learning to say no when needed, staying consistent with routines, and being kinder to myself are areas I really need to work on.

-->

Future Change


Moving forward, I plan to apply the techniques I’ve learned in simple and consistent ways. I will start by setting daily priorities using a planner, practicing deep breathing when I feel anxious, and taking small breaks during long work sessions. I also want to include mindful activities like journaling, sky-watching, or talking to close friends to manage my emotions better. These positive habits will not only help reduce my stress but also improve my focus, confidence, and overall lifestyle.
